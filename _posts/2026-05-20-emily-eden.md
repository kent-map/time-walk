Dickens to the Hon. Miss Emily Eden. 25 May 1857.[^ref1]

‘As to the time when I had the pleasure of talking with you at Broadstairs[^ref2], you know how certain particular scenes will sometimes fix themselves in the mind. Inseparable from my remembrance of that coast, and I verily believe from my abstract idea of a Lighthouse, is an Autumn afternoon in a little garden full of red geraniums, on the road to the North Foreland – you on your couch – Lord Clarendon on one side of it – myself on the other – a bright sky over the cornfields, and the ships sailing away in the distance. All this has, ever since that hour, been still and unchangeable in my memory; - got itself daguerrotyped there in some indelible / way, and has gone up Swiss mountains with me and gone floating about Venice and gone drumming and dancing about France, as distinct as ever, everywhere.’
